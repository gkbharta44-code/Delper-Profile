The Mission

Humanitarian by architecture, not by intention

Every commerce platform built before Delper has extracted value from the people at the base of the economic pyramid — charging them to transact, charging them to access, charging them to participate. Delper inverts this. The supply chain that has always taxed the consumer invisibly, the advertisers who have always profited from consumer attention, and the token traders who benefit from consumer-driven liquidity all pay into a single pool, returned as income to the person actually using the platform.

"The decision to charge the supply chain rather than the user is the founding humanitarian act. It cannot be removed without destroying the business model entirely — human welfare and commercial return are not in tension. They are the same variable."

Renewal, Not Abandonment

What happens to the city residents leave behind

When a population relocates to a completed smart city, the legacy city they leave is not abandoned. Delper reclaims and retrofits it — a second humanitarian commitment, run in one-for-one parity with the smart-city programme, so that renewal follows relocation everywhere it happens.

Capital Payback

How long until 4,400 cities pay for themselves

Total construction capital for 2,200 smart cities at $306.25B each: $673.75T. Total retrofit investment for 2,200 reclaimed cities at $306.5B each: $674.30T (financed separately via 25-year MTNs). At full Y21 revenue of $145.49T/yr across all five streams, the smart-city capital is recovered well within the 21-year programme window once all streams are operational.

Digital + Employment + Supply Chain Only (3-stream baseline)

Narrow revenue base falls short of debt service

Digital ad revenue ($10.62T/yr) plus supply chain (6% fee + 12% profit = 18% combined, $13.86T/yr) plus employment ($6.42T/yr) gives a combined $30.90T/yr at terminal state — well short of the $66.65T/yr required to service the corporate debt stack on its own.

Full 5-Stream Revenue Stack

$145.49T/yr at Y21 — 2.18× debt service

Adding smart-city rental ($47.16T/yr) and retrofit yield ($67.43T/yr) delivers $145.49T/yr total by Y21. Required annual debt service on a 25-year FSG loan at 1.8% covering the $1,332.41T total debt stack is $66.65T/yr — covered at 2.18× at full operational scale. Corporate DSCR: 2.26× (Y21 actual) / 2.18× (full steady-state).

The retrofit programme ($306.5B × 2,200 cities = $674.30T) is financed separately via 25-year Medium Term Notes and is not included in the smart-city debt service calculation above. Retrofit MTN DSCR: 199,935×.

Financing Structure

Would a sovereign-guaranteed loan actually cover this?

A payback period isn't the same question as a loan's serviceability. A loan carries scheduled annual debt service — principal and interest — so what matters is whether yearly revenue covers that payment, not just whether cumulative revenue eventually exceeds total capital.

Why Rental Income is the Decisive Factor

Smart city rental ($47.16T/yr) tips the balance

Smart city rental at 7% yield on $306.25B per city delivers $21.44B/city/yr — $47.16T/yr across 2,200 cities at Y21. Without this stream plus retrofit yield, digital + employment + supply chain revenue ($30.90T/yr) covers less than half of required debt service. With them, total revenue covers debt service at 2.18× and the programme is financeable on standard 25-year sovereign guarantee terms.

A Realistic Tenor with Real-World Precedent

25 years — fits standard sovereign guarantee practice

Sovereign guarantees on infrastructure debt typically max out around 25–40 years, matching real concession periods. The 25-year structure needs no political-continuity exception, covers debt service at 2.18× once all five revenue streams are counted, and aligns with the Full Sovereign Guarantee (FSG) model documented throughout this programme.
